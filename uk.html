UK Student Visa

ABOUT UK:

The United Kingdom (UK) is a historic and culturally rich nation located in northwestern Europe. Comprising England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland, it boasts a blend of vibrant cities and picturesque countryside. London, its capital, is a global hub for finance, art, and history, home to landmarks like Big Ben and the Tower of London. The UK is renowned for its contributions to literature, science, and politics, producing figures like Shakespeare and Newton. Its universities, including Oxford and Cambridge, are world-leading. With a rich heritage and modern innovation, the UK remains a global powerhouse with immense influence on the world stage.

ABOUT UK'S EDUCATION:

The United Kingdom is renowned for its world-class education system, attracting students from across the globe. It offers a diverse range of programs and qualifications, from GCSEs and A-levels to undergraduate and postgraduate degrees. The UK is home to prestigious institutions known for its academic excellence and cutting-edge research. Education emphasizes critical thinking, creativity, and innovation, preparing students for global challenges. With opportunities for scholarships, diverse cultural exposure, and internationally recognized qualifications, studying in the UK opens doors to endless possibilities. Its education system is a blend of tradition, modernity, and academic rigor.


Bachelor's, master's, and doctorate degrees are part of the UK's higher education. An international student may apply to colleges through pathways programmes that involve foundation courses and English language training.

Key Criteria for Studying in the United Kingdom

1. Academic Eligibility
  • 1. International students in the UK are permitted to work while pursuing their studies:
    • During Term Time: Up to 20 hours per week (part-time).
    • During Holidays: Over 30 hours per week (full-time).
2. Scholarship

There are several ways in which British institutions provide financial assistance to overseas students, which in turn helps to pay for things like course fees and living expenses. The amount of financial aid granted to students entirely relies on the programme they enroll in and the university they pick.

3. Visa Process and Requirements:

The visa application is evaluated under the new point based standards. To get a student visa, you need to acquire 40 points. 30 points are granted for your confirmation of acceptance for studies (CAS) number and the original documents submitted to get the CAS latter.

Your bank statement stating that you have sufficient money to meet the maintenance cost to give you the additional 10 points.

In order to qualify for a Tier 4 Student Visa, you need to have the following:

  • An unconditional offer letter, i.e. the CAS letter of a place where you intend to study your desired programme
  • Pass the necessary english proficiency requirements and produce a document for the same
  • Provide sufficient funds by way of your bank statement stating that you will be able to sustain yourself and pay for your course

4. Visa processing time:

The UK Student Visa processing time is divided into 3 categories.

  • Super Priority - Visa shall be granted within 24 business hours of applying.
  • Priority Visa - Priority Visa applicants are granted visa within 3-5 business days.
  • Normal Visa - Normal visa applications usually take 3 weeks to grant the visa.

5. English Proficiency Requirements:

  • IELTS
    • Exemption: No IELTS required if:
      • You scored 60% or higher in 12th-grade English, OR
      • You provide a Medium of Instruction (MOI) certificate stating that your previous education was conducted in English.
    • Postgraduate:
      • Band: 6.5 (minimum 5.5) or 6.0 (minimum 5.5).
    • Undergraduate:
      • Band: 6.0 (minimum 5.5).
  • PTE
    • Overall: 52 (minimum 52 per module).
  • UKVI English
    • Band: 5.5–6.5 (minimum 5.5 for all levels).
  • UKVI IELTS
    • Band: 5.5 (minimum 5.5 per module).
  • TOEFL
    • Score: 60–90.

6. UK Visa Process

  • Points-Based System (40 Points)
    • 30 Points:
      • Issued for Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) and related documents.
    • 10 Points:
      • Awarded for financial proof (e.g., bank statement).

7. Eligibility Checklist

  • CAS Letter: Proof of admission into a recognized UK institution.
  • English Proficiency: Valid IELTS, TOEFL, PTE scores, or MOI certificate.
  • Financial Evidence: Bank statement proving funds for tuition and living expenses.

8. Visa Processing Time

  • Super Priority: Decision in 24 hours.
  • Priority Visa: Processed in 3–5 business days.
  • Standard Visa: Takes up to 3 weeks.
